Consensus Summary
=================

Following the five trials, a core consensus emerged across all agents regarding the need for careful integration of AI tools in education, emphasizing that these tools should primarily serve to augment, not replace, students’ critical thinking and problem-solving abilities. Despite this agreement, a significant point of contention remained regarding the degree of risk posed by AI, with Bob and David consistently expressing greater concern about potential cognitive harm than Alice and Carol. Notably, David demonstrated the most significant shift in stance, moving from initially supportive to a more cautious perspective focused on moderation and deliberate strategies. Ultimately, the group concluded that AI tools can be valuable learning aids, but only when implemented with a rigorous commitment to fostering independent thought and critical evaluation skills within the educational environment.
